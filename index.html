Known past
My academic path towards User Interface/User Experience (UI/UX) design at the University of Latvia has been an unexpected adventure. Fueled by a passion for creative expression that blossomed in my early art classes.
During my early years, art class was my sanctuary. From imaginative landscapes to detailed characters, I reveled in the freedom of visual communication. 
I initially gravitated towards design fields. Sketchpads were my playgrounds, where I reveled in crafting imaginative landscapes and detailed characters.

First step

As I entered high school, the question of career aspirations arose. Graphic design seemed the natural path, and I envisioned myself crafting logos and websites that would enhance the digital world.

While my artistic inclinations drew me initially to design fields, a deeper dive into UI/UX revealed the crucial role of program engineering principles in creating not just visually appealing interfaces but also ones that function seamlessly and cater to user needs.

Following this trajectory, I enrolled in the design program at Riga Technical University (RTU). While I thrived in the world of typography, color theory, and a little about UI/UX design fundamentals, a growing curiosity emerged – a desire to understand the "why" behind web design.
A New beginning
© Image was made by page author - Arina Fokina
This yearning for deeper understanding led to a pivotal shift. I realized that while UI/UX design relies heavily on creativity, a strong foundation in engineering is essential to bridge the gap between visual appeal and technical functionality. This realization steered me towards the Engineering program at the University of Latvia. 
What University gives
flexibility
The University of Latvia offering the opportunity to study more than one program, and select between Bachelor's and professional studies, provides the perfect environment for my evolving academic interests, given its emphasis on computer science and information technology.  
opportunity
Now, I can not only hone my creative design skills but also gain the technical expertise necessary to build user interfaces and comprehend their user interaction patterns. This combination empowers me to approach UI/UX design from a holistic perspective, ensuring both aesthetic appeal and a user-centric functionality. 
Future
My journey has been one of unexpected twists and exciting discoveries. Studying Program Engineering at LU allows me to embrace both my creative sensibilities and my technical aptitude. In this dynamic environment, I am confident that I can develop the skills and knowledge necessary to become a successful UI/UX designer, crafting interfaces that are not just visually captivating but also technically sound and profoundly user-friendly.
